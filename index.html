RUTH BADER GINSBURG

1933 - 2020


The Great Dissenter

Ruth Bader Ginsburg during an NPR interview in 2019

Ruth Bader Ginsburg (affectionately known as RBG) has been a champion for equal rights during and before her time as Supreme Court justice. A few of these highlights include:

  • 1972-1980 - Professor at Columbia University School of Law. She was the first woman to be hired with tenure at Columbia Law.
  • 1973-1980 - General counsel for the ACLU, founding ACLU's Women's Rights Project
  • 1993 - RBG was sworn in as the second woman, and first Jewish person, to serve on the US Supreme Court
  • 2002 - Inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame
  • 2013 - Becomes the first Supreme Court justice to officiate a same-sex marriage ceremony

Fun facts about RBG:

  • Her nickname is "Notorious RBG"
  • Her workout routine has inspired a book and a hilarious TV appearance with Stephen Colbert
"When I'm sometimes asked 'When will there be enough (women on the Supreme Court)?' and my answer is: 'When there are nine.' People are shocked. But there'd been nine men, and nobody's ever raised a question about that." - RBG
